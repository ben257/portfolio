Fourier Series Visualization

About

A Fourier series is an expansion of a periodic function into a sum of trigonometric functions. We visualize the $n^{\text{th}}$ partial sum of the Fourier series, where the sum includes $n$ frequencies. The inputted function is converted to a periodic function, with the inputted period, and approximated by the partial sum. The $N^{\text{th}}$ partial sum is given by: $f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{N} \left( a_n \cos(\frac{2 \pi n x}{P}) + b_n \sin(\frac{2 \pi n x}{P}) \right)$ The coefficients of the Fourier Series are defined by: \begin{align} a_0 &= \frac{1}{P} \int_P f(x) \, dx \\ a_n &= \frac{2}{P} \int_P f(x) \cos\left(2\pi \frac{n}{P} x\right) dx, \quad \text{for } n \geq 1 \\ b_n &= \frac{2}{P} \int_P f(x) \sin\left(2\pi \frac{n}{P} x\right) dx, \quad \text{for } n \geq 1 \end{align} For even functions, the Fourier series should only consist of even terms, and so the coefficients of the $\sin$ terms, $b_n$ will be zero. And for odd functions, the coefficients of the $cos$ terms, $a_n$ will be zero.
